# Company Brief

## Overview

Company name → one-page brief a person can absorb in five minutes before the meeting. Everything cited, public sources only, recency-weighted — a 2022 fact presented as current is how briefs embarrass their owners.

## When to Use

- Pre-call/pre-demo prep, account enrichment, partner or vendor due-diligence lite
- NOT for: deep financial due diligence, or profiling private individuals — people appear only in their public professional roles

## Process

1. **Disambiguate first.** Confirm the exact company (domain is the anchor) — name collisions are common. Note what the user's relationship to them is (selling, partnering, competing) — it shapes the talking points.
2. **Sweep public sources** with the available search/fetch tools: official site (product, pricing, about), recent news (last 6–12 months), funding/size signals (press releases, public registries, LinkedIn-style headcount claims), job postings (what they're building/struggling with), reviews of their product, founders'/execs' public statements.
3. **Date and source every fact.** Each claim in the brief carries (source, date). Conflicting numbers across sources → show the range, don't average silently. Nothing found on a point → "no public signal", which is itself information.
4. **Compose the brief** (≤1 page):
   - **Snapshot:** what they do for whom, one sentence; founded, HQ, size estimate, funding stage — each dated
   - **Money & momentum:** how they earn, pricing model, growth/decline signals
   - **Recent moves:** 3–5 dated events (launches, hires, raises, incidents) newest-first
   - **Signals for you:** tech stack hints, hiring focus, stated priorities — filtered for relevance to the user's goal
   - **People:** meeting counterparts and relevant execs — role, tenure, public statements only
   - **Talking points:** 3 openers tied to fresh specifics, and 1–2 landmines to avoid (recent layoffs, a public incident, a competitor they hate)
5. **Batch mode** (account enrichment): same fields, one row per company, talking points column shortened to one hook.

## Verification

- [ ] Every fact has source + date; no undated "currently"
- [ ] Company identity confirmed by domain — no mixed-in facts from a same-named firm
- [ ] Talking points reference specifics from this brief, not generic flattery
- [ ] People covered in professional capacity only
